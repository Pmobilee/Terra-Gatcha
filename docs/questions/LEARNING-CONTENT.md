# Learning & Content — 50 Questions for Roadmap Expansion

> These questions explore the knowledge system, quiz mechanics, fact pipeline,
> GAIA teaching, and learning retention. Answer them to refine the educational core
> of Terra Gacha. Each question references the current implementation state and
> includes professional advice from an educational game design perspective.

## A. Content Pipeline & Scaling (Questions 1-10)

### Q1: What is the initial fact ingestion source, and how do you plan to curate the first 5,000 facts?
**Context**: The roadmap (Phase 11) describes a POST `/api/facts/ingest` endpoint with LLM-generated metadata. Currently, 122 general facts and 400 N3 vocab exist in `src/data/seed/`. DD-V2-001 states "manually curated from interesting books" as the initial source, but there is no pipeline for getting from a book to a structured fact record.
**My Advice**: Build a "fact extraction prompt" that takes a highlighted passage from a book (or Wikipedia section) and outputs the full fact JSON schema in one shot. Batch these through Claude API with a reviewer-in-the-loop. Aim for 50-100 facts per curation session. Starting with Wikipedia's "Did you know" archives and books like "The Science Book" (DK) gives you high fun-score content quickly. Do NOT attempt a web crawler yet -- the editorial quality bar for "ultra-interesting" facts (per KNOWLEDGE_SYSTEM.md) requires human judgment on source material selection.

### Q2: How will you validate that LLM-generated distractors are both plausible and unambiguously wrong?
**Context**: Each fact currently has 24-25 distractors (e.g., `facts-general.json` nsci-001 has 25 distractors for the lightning temperature fact). DD-V2-004 specifies fully LLM-generated distractors. However, some existing distractors contain partial truths (e.g., "Lightning is hotter, but only for a fraction of a millisecond" -- this is arguably true, not wrong). The quiz grading in `quizService.ts` uses exact string matching (`answer === fact.correctAnswer`).
**My Advice**: Add a "distractor validation" step in the LLM pipeline where a second LLM pass reviews each distractor against the correct answer and flags any that could be argued as partially correct. Store a `distractor_confidence` score per distractor. At quiz time, only select distractors with confidence above a threshold. This prevents frustrating "wait, that IS true" moments that erode trust in the learning system. Budget 5-10% of pipeline compute for this validation pass.

### Q3: Should distractors be difficulty-tiered so the quiz can select easier or harder wrong answers based on player mastery?
**Context**: The current `quizService.ts` randomly selects 3 distractors from the pool of ~25 via `shuffleArray`. KNOWLEDGE_SYSTEM.md states "difficulty of distractors scales with the fact's inherent difficulty rating, the depth layer, and the player's mastery level" but this is not yet implemented. The `difficulty` field on facts (1-5) is stored but unused in quiz selection.
**My Advice**: Yes -- tier each distractor as "easy" (obviously wrong), "medium" (plausible), or "hard" (requires specific knowledge to eliminate). When a player first encounters a fact, serve easy distractors. As their SM-2 interval grows, serve harder ones. This is how Duolingo maintains challenge without frustration. Add a `difficulty_tier` field to each distractor in the generation pipeline. The 25-distractor pool gives you roughly 8 easy, 10 medium, 7 hard -- enough variety at each tier.

### Q4: What is the content review workflow -- who approves facts before they enter the player-facing database?
**Context**: DD-V2-005 mentions "fact editor, browser, and approval tool" as a web dashboard. DD-V2-006 says "Wikipedia with a citation URL is sufficient for v1 verification." Phase 11.4 describes a management dashboard. But there is no defined approval workflow: who reviews, what the approval criteria are, or how rejected facts are handled.
**My Advice**: Define three states: `draft` (LLM-generated, not player-visible), `approved` (reviewed, player-visible), and `archived` (rejected/replaced). For a solo developer, you ARE the reviewer. Build a simple "review queue" in the dashboard that shows 10 facts at a time with approve/reject buttons plus a "fix and re-generate" option. Prioritize reviewing distractor quality and age-rating flags. At scale (10,000+ facts), consider community review with a trust system where players with high mastery in a category can flag inaccurate facts.

### Q5: How will you handle fact accuracy disputes when a player believes a "correct" answer is wrong?
**Context**: KNOWLEDGE_SYSTEM.md mentions `source_url` and `source_name` fields for fact verification. The current schema in `types.ts` has `sourceName` but no `sourceUrl`. There is no in-game mechanism for players to flag inaccurate facts. DD-V2-006 says Wikipedia is sufficient for v1.
**My Advice**: Add an in-game "Report this fact" button on the fact detail card and on the post-quiz wrong-answer screen. This is critical for educational credibility. Log reports with the player's argument (free text, max 200 chars). When a fact accumulates 3+ reports, auto-flag it for review. Science evolves -- Pluto was a planet, then it was not. Your fact database must be a living document. Also add `sourceUrl` to the schema now. Players who see "Source: NASA" and can tap through to NASA.gov will trust the entire system more.

### Q6: How should the 25-distractor generation handle facts where there are fewer than 25 plausible wrong answers in the domain?
**Context**: Most general knowledge facts (e.g., "how many hearts does an octopus have?") have limited plausible number-based distractors. The current facts show padding with implausible options like "The comparison only holds during thunderstorms" (nsci-001). Language vocab is easier -- there are thousands of plausible wrong translations. But for niche facts, forcing 25 distractors dilutes quality.
**My Advice**: Set a minimum of 12 high-quality distractors, not 25, as the hard floor. 12 distractors still give you 220 unique 3-distractor combinations, meaning a player would need to see the same fact 220 times before seeing a repeated distractor set. For facts where the LLM can produce 25+ quality distractors, keep them. Add a `distractor_count` field and adjust `getQuizChoices` to handle variable pool sizes. Quality over quantity -- 12 excellent distractors beat 25 padded ones.

### Q7: What is the target ratio of facts across the 7 top-level categories, and how will gap analysis drive content creation?
**Context**: Current categories in `types.ts` are: Language, Natural Sciences, Life Sciences, History, Geography, Technology, Culture. The 122 general facts are not evenly distributed. Phase 11.4 mentions "gap analysis view" in the dashboard. The interest system (Phase 12) will weight spawning toward player preferences, but an unbalanced database limits what can be weighted.
**My Advice**: Do NOT aim for equal distribution. Aim for "minimum viable depth" per category: at least 200 facts in each top-level category before launch (5,000 total = ~700 per category average). Within categories, chase the long tail -- obscure subcategories like "Mycology" or "Numismatics" are what make completionists obsessive. Use the gap analysis tool to identify subcategories with fewer than 20 facts and batch-generate for those specifically. The Knowledge Tree looks most compelling when it has visible branches to grow.

### Q8: Should facts have an expiration date or a "freshness" flag for facts that may become outdated?
**Context**: Facts about population counts, world records, political leaders, or technology achievements can become stale. "The world's tallest building is the Burj Khalifa" may not be true forever. There is no `lastVerified` or `expirationDate` field in the current schema.
**My Advice**: Add a `content_volatility` enum: `timeless` (octopus hearts), `slow_change` (world records, population), `current_events` (leaders, policies -- avoid these entirely for v1). Timeless facts are the backbone; slow-change facts get flagged for annual review. This is a small schema addition now that prevents a large credibility problem later. For v1, focus 90% of content on timeless facts. They are more fun anyway -- "lightning is 5x hotter than the sun" will never expire.

### Q9: How will you handle the transition from the current client-side sql.js fact database to a server-side PostgreSQL fact database?
**Context**: Currently, `scripts/build-facts-db.mjs` reads seed JSON files and writes `public/facts.db` (sql.js, client-side). `factsDB.ts` loads this at startup. Phase 19 plans PostgreSQL for production. Phase 11 introduces a server-side fact ingestion API. This means the fact source of truth will move from bundled JSON to a remote database.
**My Advice**: Design this as a three-phase migration: (1) Server becomes source of truth for fact content; client downloads a "fact pack" on first launch and periodically syncs. (2) Client caches facts locally for offline play using the existing sql.js infrastructure as a cache layer, not the source. (3) The `build-facts-db.mjs` script becomes a "seed pack builder" for offline-first new installs. The key architectural decision: facts are fetched in batches (e.g., 50 at a time) and the client quiz service works entirely from the local cache. Never require a network call to show a quiz.

### Q10: Should facts support multimedia beyond pixel art -- audio clips, short video, or interactive diagrams?
**Context**: The fact schema has `audio_url` (for language pronunciation) and `imageUrl` (for pixel art). DD-V2-028 says audio-based and image-based quizzes are "nice-to-have for the far future." But the current system is entirely text + static image.
**My Advice**: For v1, keep it text + pixel art only. Multimedia dramatically increases content pipeline complexity, storage costs, and loading times on mobile. The one exception: language learning pronunciation audio, which is non-negotiable for any serious language learning feature (Phase 24). When you DO add audio, use a TTS service (like ElevenLabs or Azure TTS) to auto-generate pronunciation clips rather than recording them manually. For general knowledge facts, pixel art illustrations with the greyscale-to-color mastery progression are more than sufficient and thematically cohesive.

## B. SM-2 Algorithm & Quiz Mechanics (Questions 11-20)

### Q11: The current SM-2 implementation uses a binary correct/wrong mapping (quality 5 for correct, quality 1 for wrong). Should you add granularity for "hard correct" vs "easy correct"?
**Context**: `sm2.ts` line 38 sets `quality = 5` for all correct answers and `quality = 1` for all wrong answers. The study session UI (`StudySession.svelte`) has "Got it" / "Didn't get it" buttons. KNOWLEDGE_SYSTEM.md mentions a possible "that was easy / that was hard" modifier. The SM-2 algorithm's ease factor formula (`0.1 - (5 - quality) * (0.08 + (5 - quality) * 0.02)`) is designed for the full 0-5 quality range but currently only sees 1 or 5.
**My Advice**: Add a third button: "Easy" (quality=5), "Got it" (quality=4), and "Didn't get it" (quality=1). Three options is the sweet spot -- Anki's original 4-button system (Again/Hard/Good/Easy) is well-validated but can feel overwhelming for casual players. "Easy" pushes the interval out faster (the ease factor increases more), "Got it" is the standard progression, and "Didn't get it" resets. This is a small UI change with a large impact on scheduling accuracy. Do NOT add this to in-mine quizzes (those stay binary) -- only in voluntary study sessions where the player has time to self-assess.

### Q12: The SM-2 first interval jumps from 1 day to 6 days (repetitions 1 to 2). Is this too aggressive for a mobile game where players may skip several days?
**Context**: `sm2.ts` lines 41-45 set interval=1 for first correct, interval=6 for second correct, then `interval * easeFactor` thereafter. Standard SM-2 uses these exact intervals. But mobile game players are less disciplined than Anki users -- they may play Monday, skip to Thursday, then play Saturday. A 6-day gap after only 2 exposures risks a high lapse rate.
**My Advice**: Shorten the second interval to 3 days instead of 6. This is a well-known SM-2 modification used by Mnemosyne and other apps targeting casual learners. The logic: after only 2 correct reviews, memory consolidation is fragile. A 3-day interval gives one more early reinforcement before the exponential growth kicks in. After the 3rd correct (repetitions=3), the standard `interval * easeFactor` takes over. Change line 44 from `interval = 6` to `interval = 3`. Monitor lapse rates and adjust.

### Q13: How should the consistency penalty system work for facts the player has never studied in a calm setting but encounters as a pop quiz in the mine?
**Context**: `BALANCE.CONSISTENCY_PENALTY_O2: 8` applies when a player gets a fact wrong in the mine that they previously got right with 2+ repetitions (`CONSISTENCY_MIN_REPS: 2`). But the current QuizManager also serves brand-new facts as pop quizzes. If a player encounters a new fact as a pop quiz and gets it wrong, they should not be penalized the same way as forgetting a "learned" fact.
**My Advice**: The consistency penalty should ONLY apply to facts where `reviewState.repetitions >= CONSISTENCY_MIN_REPS` AND the fact was last reviewed in a study session (not another pop quiz). Add a `lastReviewContext: 'study' | 'mine' | 'ritual'` field to ReviewState. The philosophy: if you studied it calmly and still forgot under mild pressure, the SM-2 state was inflated. If you only ever saw it as a random pop quiz, there was never a calm baseline to be "inconsistent" with.

### Q14: Should mastery thresholds be different for general facts vs language vocabulary?
**Context**: `getMasteryLevel` in `sm2.ts` uses fixed interval thresholds: learning (<3 days), familiar (<10), known (<30), mastered (30+). KNOWLEDGE_SYSTEM.md says mastered is 60+ days. Language vocabulary benefits from more frequent review than general facts -- you need to recall a word faster than you need to recall that Neptune has fast winds.
**My Advice**: Yes, create two mastery profiles. For general facts: new(0), learning(1-3d), familiar(4-14d), known(15-60d), mastered(60d+). For language vocabulary: new(0), learning(1-2d), familiar(3-7d), known(8-30d), mastered(30d+). The rationale: vocabulary mastery means instant recall (you need to read/speak fluently), while fact mastery means reliable long-term retention. The Knowledge Tree should visually distinguish these via different leaf shapes or icons -- a word leaf vs a fact leaf.

### Q15: Should quiz questions ever be timed during dives, or is "no time pressure" absolute?
**Context**: KNOWLEDGE_SYSTEM.md explicitly states: "No time pressure on quizzes -- this is a learning game, not a speed test." But during dives, oxygen is constantly ticking down. A player who spends 60 seconds thinking about a quiz answer IS being time-pressured by the oxygen system, even without a visible timer.
**My Advice**: Pause oxygen drain during quiz overlays. This is non-negotiable if you want quizzes to genuinely be learning moments and not speed tests. If oxygen drains during quizzes, players will tap the first plausible answer instead of thinking carefully, which defeats the entire educational purpose. Check whether the current implementation already pauses oxygen. If not, this is a high-priority bug. The one exception: quiz gates in deeper layers could have a VISIBLE but generous timer (60 seconds) to create dramatic tension -- but this should be a Phase 17 addictiveness-pass decision, not the default.

### Q16: How should the quiz system handle the "learning" phase for brand-new facts the player has never seen before?
**Context**: `selectQuestion` in `quizService.ts` falls back to a random fact from the full pool when no facts are due for review. This means a player can be quizzed on a fact they have NEVER encountered. In the mine, this creates a guessing situation, not a learning situation. The artifact ingestion flow (fact reveal at base) is meant to be the first exposure.
**My Advice**: Separate "quiz" from "teach." When a player encounters a never-before-seen fact as a pop quiz, it should be a "teaching moment" -- show the question, let them answer (or guess), then ALWAYS reveal the full fact card with wow factor, explanation, and GAIA comment regardless of whether they got it right. This first exposure is not graded by SM-2. It enters the review system only after this initial teaching moment. For quiz gates, only use facts the player has seen at least once (even if just in a previous pop quiz). This prevents frustrating "how was I supposed to know that?" moments.

### Q17: The current random quiz chance is 4% per block (`RANDOM_QUIZ_CHANCE: 0.04`). DD-V2-027 says increase to 8-10%. How should quiz frequency adapt to player behavior?
**Context**: A fixed 8-10% quiz rate means roughly one quiz per 10-12 blocks mined. At an average pace, this is roughly one quiz per 30-60 seconds of mining. For fast players or long sessions, this could feel intrusive. For slow players or short sessions, they might only see 2-3 quizzes per dive.
**My Advice**: Make the quiz rate adaptive with a hidden cooldown. Set the BASE rate at 8%, but add a minimum interval of 15 blocks (or ~45 seconds) between quizzes. Additionally, track "quiz fatigue" -- after 5+ quizzes in a single dive, decrease the trigger chance by 2% per additional quiz. Reset on layer descent. This creates a rhythm: frequent enough to learn, spaced enough to maintain mining flow. Also: the first quiz in any dive should NOT trigger until at least 10 blocks are mined (currently `RANDOM_QUIZ_MIN_BLOCKS: 5` -- double it), so the player gets into a flow state before being interrupted.

### Q18: Should wrong answers during dives always cost oxygen, or should some quiz contexts be risk-free to encourage guessing on new material?
**Context**: Current penalties: quiz gate wrong = 10 O2 (`QUIZ_GATE_FAILURE_OXYGEN_COST`), random pop quiz wrong = 5 O2 (`RANDOM_QUIZ_WRONG_O2_COST`), consistency penalty = 8 O2 extra. These penalties create risk aversion around quizzes. A player might prefer NOT to encounter quizzes, which conflicts with the "quizzes should feel like a game mechanic, not homework" design goal.
**My Advice**: Create two quiz categories: "review quizzes" (testing known facts) and "discovery quizzes" (first exposure to new facts). Review quizzes carry O2 penalties because the player SHOULD know them. Discovery quizzes are REWARD-ONLY: correct = bonus dust/oxygen, wrong = no penalty but shows the answer with explanation. This makes encountering new facts exciting (free learning opportunity!) rather than risky. Psychologically, this also reduces the sting of the consistency penalty on review quizzes because the player accepted the "I should know this" framing.

### Q19: How should quiz gate difficulty scale across the 20 mine layers?
**Context**: DD-V2-022 specifies 20 layers with mandatory quiz gates per layer. Current quiz selection (`selectQuestion`) picks the oldest due fact or a random one -- no difficulty consideration. The `difficulty` field (1-5) exists on facts but is unused in selection. Layer 1 should be easier than layer 15.
**My Advice**: Map layer number to a difficulty range: layers 1-5 select from difficulty 1-2 facts, layers 6-10 from 2-3, layers 11-15 from 3-4, and layers 16-20 from 4-5. Additionally, in deeper layers, select harder distractors from the tiered distractor pool (see Q3). This creates an "intellectual depth" that mirrors physical depth -- the deeper you go, the harder the questions. But always: if a player has no facts in the appropriate difficulty range, fall back to any due fact rather than showing nothing. The gate must never be impossible due to content availability.

### Q20: Should the quiz system support "partial credit" for answers that are close but not exact?
**Context**: `gradeAnswer` in `quizService.ts` uses strict equality: `answer === fact.correctAnswer`. For multiple choice this works fine. But DD-V2-028 mentions future fill-in-blank formats. For language vocab, "I understood" vs "I understand" should both be accepted for a translation of the same word.
**My Advice**: For the current multiple-choice format, exact matching is correct and sufficient -- do not change it. But add an `acceptableAnswers: string[]` field to the fact schema now for future fill-in-blank support. For language learning specifically, this field would store conjugation variants, spelling alternatives, and common acceptable translations. When fill-in-blank is implemented (far future per DD-V2-028), grade against the full `acceptableAnswers` array with fuzzy matching (Levenshtein distance <= 2). This is a schema-forward decision that costs nothing now.

## C. GAIA as Teacher & Companion (Questions 21-30)

### Q21: How much should GAIA's teaching style change based on the `gaiaMood` setting (snarky / enthusiastic / calm)?
**Context**: `gaiaDialogue.ts` has 9 trigger pools x 3 moods. The mood affects tone but currently does not affect pedagogical approach. For example, when a player gets a fact wrong, snarky GAIA and enthusiastic GAIA both show the same `explanation` field -- only the GAIA comment differs.
**My Advice**: The mood should affect DELIVERY but not CONTENT. All moods should show the explanation, the mnemonic, and the correct answer. What changes is GAIA's framing: snarky ("Seriously? The answer was right there. Here is why..."), enthusiastic ("Ooh, tricky one! Let me help you remember..."), calm ("No worries. Here is the key detail to focus on..."). Generate three `gaiaWrongComment` variants per fact in the LLM pipeline (one per mood). This is a relatively cheap generation step that dramatically improves the companion feel.

### Q22: GAIA currently offers mnemonics only when a player "struggles" with a fact. What defines "struggling" and when exactly should mnemonics appear?
**Context**: KNOWLEDGE_SYSTEM.md says "GAIA offers mnemonics when you struggle with a fact (gets it wrong multiple times)." The `mnemonic` field exists on the Fact type but is optional and unpopulated in the current seed data. The current implementation shows the `explanation` field as a "Memory Tip" on wrong answers.
**My Advice**: Define "struggling" as: getting the same fact wrong 2+ times within a 7-day window, OR having a ReviewState where `repetitions` reset from 3+ back to 0 (meaning they knew it well and forgot it). When struggling is detected, show the mnemonic prominently on the fact card and in the quiz wrong-answer screen. Pre-populate the mnemonic field for ALL facts in the LLM pipeline -- it is cheap to generate and valuable to have ready. GAIA should say "I noticed you keep missing this one. Try this trick..." -- this is Duolingo's "encouragement after failure" pattern, which has strong retention data behind it.

### Q23: Should GAIA actively suggest which facts to study next, and if so, what heuristic should drive the suggestions?
**Context**: KNOWLEDGE_SYSTEM.md mentions "GAIA suggestions weighted by interests" and Phase 15.4 describes GAIA remembering favorite categories. Currently, the study session selects due facts by SM-2 scheduling (oldest due first). GAIA does not influence study selection.
**My Advice**: Yes, but frame it as GAIA's personality, not a cold algorithm. GAIA should suggest studying based on three signals: (1) facts approaching their due date ("Your octopus fact is due tomorrow -- want to lock it in?"), (2) facts close to mastery threshold ("Two more reviews and your Neptune fact is mastered!"), and (3) new facts in the player's interest area ("I just decoded a Biology artifact -- want to learn about tardigrades?"). Show these as clickable thought bubbles in the dome, not forced prompts. The key insight: GAIA makes the SM-2 scheduling feel personal rather than algorithmic.

### Q24: How should GAIA handle the moment a player masters their first fact (reaches the mastered interval)?
**Context**: `getMasteryLevel` returns 'mastered' at 30+ day interval (sm2.ts line 108), though KNOWLEDGE_SYSTEM.md defines mastery at 60+ days. This is the most significant learning achievement in the game -- permanent retention of knowledge. Currently there is no special celebration or GAIA response for this milestone.
**My Advice**: This should be one of the BIGGEST moments in the game. When a fact transitions to "mastered": (1) Full-screen celebration animation rivaling a Legendary artifact reveal, (2) GAIA delivers a unique congratulatory message referencing the specific fact ("You have permanently learned that octopuses have three hearts. That knowledge is YOURS now. Nobody can take it away."), (3) The Knowledge Tree leaf turns golden with a visible animation, (4) First mastery ever gets an extra-special treatment (achievement unlock, unique GAIA monologue, possible reward). This is the core emotional payoff of the entire game. Under-celebrating it would be a critical design mistake.

### Q25: Should GAIA's chattiness setting (0-10) affect educational content, or only ambient/flavor dialogue?
**Context**: `settings.ts` has `gaiaChattiness` (0-10 scale). Currently this controls idle quip frequency in the dome. The question is whether a player who sets chattiness to 2 should also see fewer explanations, mnemonics, and learning tips.
**My Advice**: NEVER reduce educational content based on chattiness. Chattiness should only affect: idle quips, pet commentary, joke frequency, ambient observations, and post-dive banter. Wrong-answer explanations, mnemonics, mastery celebrations, and learning tips are ALWAYS shown at full detail regardless of chattiness. The rationale: a player who finds GAIA chatty is annoyed by flavor text, not by learning content. If they are playing a knowledge game, they want the knowledge. Add a separate "show explanations" toggle in settings for the rare player who truly wants minimal interruption, but default it to ON and do not tie it to chattiness.

### Q26: How should GAIA's "journey memory" (Phase 15.4) be implemented -- scripted templates or LLM-generated at runtime?
**Context**: DD-V2-046 says GAIA references specific facts, congratulates milestones, and remembers favorites. Phase 15.4 says "extensive scripted dialogue templates with variable interpolation (LLM integration as future enhancement)." This is an architectural decision with significant implications for personality quality vs cost.
**My Advice**: Start with scripted templates with variable interpolation -- it is cheaper, faster, and more predictable. Example template: "Remember when you learned that {factStatement}? That was back when your {category} branch only had {countAtTime} leaves!" Store ~50 templates per trigger type (milestone, category mention, streak reference, etc.) and randomly select + interpolate. The variables (factStatement, category, count) come from the player's save data. LLM generation at runtime is overkill for v1 and introduces latency, cost, and unpredictable output. Revisit LLM generation when you have the analytics to know which template types players engage with most.

### Q27: Should GAIA ever be "wrong" about a fact to create a teaching moment, or must she always be the authoritative source?
**Context**: GAIA is described as "enthusiastic, slightly obsessive about facts, and endearingly nerdy." Her personality could support occasional playful ignorance ("I THINK the answer is Jupiter, but... hmm, actually I am not sure. What do YOU think?"). This is a known pedagogical technique called "productive uncertainty."
**My Advice**: Do NOT make GAIA wrong about facts. In an educational game, the AI companion is the authority figure. If GAIA is sometimes wrong, players will distrust ALL GAIA information, including explanations and mnemonics. The "productive uncertainty" technique works in classrooms with teachers who can read the room -- it fails in automated systems. Instead, let GAIA express genuine curiosity: "I KNEW octopuses had multiple hearts, but the exact number always gets me excited. Three! How amazing is that?" This models enthusiasm for learning without undermining authority.

### Q28: How should GAIA respond to a player who repeatedly gets the same fact wrong (5+ times)?
**Context**: The SM-2 system resets interval to 1 day on each wrong answer. A player stuck on a fact will see it daily until they get it right. Currently, the same explanation and mnemonic are shown each time. There is no escalation in GAIA's teaching approach.
**My Advice**: Implement a 3-tier escalation: (1) Wrong 1-2 times: show explanation + mnemonic as now. (2) Wrong 3-4 times: GAIA offers a different angle ("Let me try explaining this differently...") -- generate 2-3 `alternateExplanation` variants per fact in the pipeline. (3) Wrong 5+ times: GAIA suggests the player tap the fact in the Knowledge Tree to "deep study" it, which opens an expanded card with the full wow factor text, the pixel art illustration, related facts, and the source link. Also flag these "chronically missed" facts in analytics -- they may indicate a confusing question/answer structure that needs editorial revision.

### Q29: Should GAIA have a "teaching mode" during study sessions that is distinct from her ambient dome personality?
**Context**: Study sessions (`StudySession.svelte`) have a calm aesthetic (floating particles, breathing guide). GAIA's personality in the dome is quippy and snarky (even on "calm" mood). During focused study, a snarky comment about the player's pet could be jarring.
**My Advice**: Yes. Create a `study` context for GAIA's dialogue that is always warm, encouraging, and pedagogically focused regardless of the mood setting. "Study GAIA" says things like: "Take your time with this one," "You are building strong connections in your memory," and "That is 7 in a row -- your brain is really cooking." The mood setting should still inflect the tone (snarky study GAIA is dryly encouraging, enthusiastic study GAIA is effusively cheerful), but the content switches from ambient/flavor to educational/supportive. This mirrors how a good teacher changes demeanor from the hallway to the classroom.

### Q30: GAIA's `gaiaComment` is currently generated once and stored per fact. Should these rotate to prevent staleness on repeated reviews?
**Context**: Each fact has a single `gaiaComment` string in the seed data (e.g., "Five times hotter than the Sun. And you are down here worried about a little lava."). A player mastering a fact will see this same comment during every review and artifact ingestion. Over 10+ exposures, it becomes noise.
**My Advice**: Generate 3-5 `gaiaComments` per fact (stored as an array, not a single string) and rotate through them. The first is the "ingestion" comment (most dramatic, for the artifact reveal). The rest are "review" comments (shorter, varied). Example for nsci-001: ingestion: "Five times hotter than the Sun!" / review 1: "Still hot." / review 2: "Your lightning knowledge sparks joy." / review 3: "Fun fact: you know this one cold. Or should I say... hot." This is a low-cost generation step that dramatically improves repeated-exposure feel. Cost: ~4x more GAIA comment tokens per fact. Value: every review feels slightly fresh.

## D. Knowledge Tree, Personalization & Progression (Questions 31-40)

### Q31: The Knowledge Tree currently uses SVG rendering with category pills. Phase 13 proposes an "Obsidian-like interactive graph." What level of interactivity is realistic for a mobile-first game?
**Context**: Phase 13.2 describes a force-directed or hierarchical graph with pan, zoom, pinch, search, and smooth animations. Open question Q-V2-4 asks about D3.js vs Cytoscape.js vs custom SVG. The current SVG implementation in `KnowledgeTree.svelte` works but is static.
**My Advice**: For mobile, force-directed graphs are a UX nightmare -- small screens, fat fingers, nodes overlapping, slow rendering with 1000+ nodes. Instead, build a HIERARCHICAL RADIAL TREE (trunk at center, branches radiating outward) with tap-to-zoom-into-branch. Think of it as a zoom-in-zoom-out navigation, not a pan-and-drag graph. Use a custom SVG/Canvas renderer, not a graph library -- libraries add 100KB+ bundle size for features you will not use. The "Obsidian feel" comes from smooth zoom transitions and the connection lines between related facts, not from force-directed physics. At 5,000+ facts, performance will be the constraint -- pre-compute layout positions and render only visible nodes.

### Q32: How should the Knowledge Tree handle the transition from 522 facts to 10,000+ without becoming visually overwhelming?
**Context**: At 522 facts, the tree has ~100 leaves across 7 branches. At 10,000 facts, it would have 10,000 leaf nodes. Even with subcategory clustering, this is too many to display simultaneously. The current KnowledgeTree component renders all nodes.
**My Advice**: Implement "level of detail" rendering. At full zoom-out (whole tree visible), show only main branches and sub-branches as thick colored lines with completion percentage labels -- no individual leaf nodes. Tap a branch to zoom into it and see sub-branches with their percentages. Tap a sub-branch to see individual leaf nodes. This 3-level zoom (forest / branch / leaf) keeps the UI clean at any content scale. A player should be able to see their OVERALL progress at a glance and drill into specifics on demand. Think Google Maps: continent -> country -> city -> street.

### Q33: Should the Knowledge Tree show facts the player has NOT yet discovered (greyed out "unknown" nodes)?
**Context**: Phase 13.3 says toggle between "all available facts" and "learned only." KNOWLEDGE_SYSTEM.md says "Undiscovered facts show as gray silhouette nodes (enticing to unlock)." DD-V2-048 says add this toggle. The question is whether showing ALL 10,000 possible facts (even as silhouettes) is motivating or demoralizing.
**My Advice**: Show unknown nodes at the BRANCH level only, not individual facts. Display "Biology: 34 learned / 312 total" on the branch, with silhouette leaf clusters representing unlearned facts. Do NOT show each individual unlearned fact as its own node -- it visually overwhelms the tree and can be demoralizing ("I only know 34 out of 312?!"). The exception: within a subcategory the player has 80%+ completion, show individual missing facts as silhouettes to drive completionist behavior. This matches the Duolingo approach of showing the next few unlockable lessons, not the entire course.

### Q34: How should the behavioral preference learning system (Phase 12.3) balance between inferred interests and explicit settings?
**Context**: DD-V2-031 says interests are weights, never hard filters (unless category-lock purchased). Phase 12.3 describes ML/heuristic inference from player behavior (keeps vs sells, study frequency, mastery speed). The risk: a player who sells all History facts because they need minerals gets their History weighting reduced, creating a reinforcing cycle.
**My Advice**: Use a decay-weighted signal model: explicit settings weight = 1.0 (constant), behavioral inference weight = 0.3 (maximum). Behavioral signals should only INCREASE category weights, never decrease them below the explicit setting baseline. Track only positive signals (voluntarily studied, kept instead of sold, high mastery speed) and ignore negative signals (selling is an economic decision, not a preference signal). Show the inferred preferences on a "GAIA thinks you like..." panel with per-category adjustment sliders. Transparency prevents the "filter bubble" problem.

### Q35: How should "mastery" be visually celebrated beyond the Knowledge Tree leaf turning gold?
**Context**: Q24 addresses the first mastery moment. But ongoing mastery events (fact #2, #10, #100 mastered) need a celebration cadence that does not become noise. Currently there is no special handling for any mastery transition.
**My Advice**: Design a tiered celebration system: mastery #1 = full-screen event (see Q24). Masteries #2-9 = GAIA comment + leaf glow + small dust bonus. Mastery #10 = mini-celebration (GAIA speech + "10 Facts Mastered!" banner + branch glow). Masteries in multiples of 25 = medium celebration. Mastery #100 = major event (unique GAIA monologue, cosmetic unlock, Knowledge Tree visual evolution -- maybe the tree grows a new physical feature). Category mastery milestones (50% of a branch, 100% of a sub-branch) get their own celebrations. The pattern: celebrate frequently at first (every mastery), then taper to milestones (every 25th) to prevent fatigue.

### Q36: Should the interest system affect which biomes appear in the mine, or only which facts/artifacts spawn?
**Context**: DD-V2-031 lists mine spawning, quiz selection, GAIA suggestions, and data disc contents as interest-affected systems. Biomes are not mentioned. But thematic connections exist: a Geology enthusiast would love the Crystal Caverns biome; a History buff would love Ancient City Ruins.
**My Advice**: Yes, but subtly. Weight biome selection so interest-aligned biomes appear ~30% more frequently, but never eliminate other biomes. This creates serendipitous discovery ("I mainly study Biology but just found an incredible History artifact in the Ancient Ruins biome!"). The biome itself does not restrict which facts appear -- all fact categories can appear in any biome. The weighting only affects the biome environment. This keeps the world feeling cohesive while gently favoring the player's interests. Do NOT make this configurable -- it should be an invisible delight, not a setting.

### Q37: How should cross-fact connections (`related_facts` field) be surfaced to the player?
**Context**: The Fact schema has `related_facts: string[]` (IDs of related facts). KNOWLEDGE_SYSTEM.md mentions GAIA commenting on connections. Phase 13.2 describes graph edges showing connections. Currently, `related_facts` is not populated in the seed data and not rendered anywhere.
**My Advice**: Surface connections at three moments: (1) When a player learns a new fact that connects to a previously mastered fact, GAIA says "This connects to what you learned about [previousFact]!" with a brief explanation of the connection. (2) In the Knowledge Tree, draw faint connection lines between related fact nodes when zoomed into a branch. (3) On the fact detail card, show a "Related" section with thumbnail links to connected facts. Populating `related_facts` should be part of the LLM pipeline (Phase 11.3) -- the LLM can easily identify semantic connections between facts in the same batch. Start by generating connections within subcategories, then expand to cross-category connections.

### Q38: Should there be a "deep dive" study mode that lets a player study all facts in a specific subcategory in sequence?
**Context**: Current study sessions pull from the global pool of due facts sorted by SM-2 schedule. There is no way to say "I want to study only Astronomy right now." The Knowledge Tree focus mode (Phase 13) could enable this, and the category-lock item (Phase 12.4) addresses long-term focus, but neither provides a one-session drill-down.
**My Advice**: Yes -- add a "Focus Study" button on each Knowledge Tree branch. Tap it to start a study session using ONLY facts from that branch (due facts first, then new facts from the branch). This is extremely valuable for students using the app for specific exam prep ("I have a biology test Thursday -- let me focus on Life Sciences"). It also drives Knowledge Tree engagement: players will tap into a branch to see what they know, then impulsively hit "Focus Study" to fill gaps. Keep the regular "Study All" as the default; Focus Study is an additional option.

### Q39: How should the game handle a player who has mastered every available fact in a category?
**Context**: At launch with ~5,000 facts and an aggressive player, category completion is achievable within months for smaller categories. When all facts in a subcategory are mastered (60+ day intervals), there are no new facts to discover and no reviews to do. The Knowledge Tree branch is fully golden.
**My Advice**: Four responses, in order: (1) A major celebration -- GAIA delivers a unique "you have mastered everything I know about [subcategory]" speech. (2) Award a permanent category badge visible on the player's profile and Knowledge Tree. (3) Unlock a "challenge mode" for that category: harder distractors, shorter answer windows, combo scoring. (4) Show a "more content coming" message and prioritize that category in the content pipeline's gap analysis. The worst response would be silence -- the player achieved something remarkable and the game should acknowledge it emphatically.

### Q40: Should the Knowledge Tree have a physical growth representation in the dome that reflects TOTAL knowledge, not just individual facts?
**Context**: DD-V2-017 and Phase 10.6 describe a clickable pixel art tree in the starter hub that "grows from sapling to massive tree." This implies visual stages based on total learned facts. The current dome has a Knowledge Tree as a menu item, not a physical object.
**My Advice**: Define 8-10 visual tree stages based on total mastered facts: sapling (0-10), seedling (11-50), young tree (51-150), growing tree (151-400), mature tree (401-1000), great tree (1001-2500), ancient tree (2501-5000), world tree (5001+). Each stage is a distinct pixel art sprite with increasing complexity (more branches, thicker trunk, visible leaves, glowing elements). Generate these via ComfyUI as a progression series. The tree should be the FIRST thing the player sees when they return to the dome -- it is the physical manifestation of their learning journey. This is one of the strongest retention hooks in the entire game.

## E. Study Design, Review Rituals & Language Learning (Questions 41-50)

### Q41: The morning (7-11 AM) and evening (7-11 PM) review ritual windows assume a specific timezone and lifestyle. How should these adapt to different players?
**Context**: `BALANCE.MORNING_REVIEW_HOUR: 7` through `EVENING_REVIEW_END: 23` define fixed windows. Players in different timezones see these relative to their device clock. But shift workers, students with afternoon classes, or players in UTC+12 have very different schedules.
**My Advice**: Let the player customize their two ritual windows in settings. Default to 7-11 AM and 7-11 PM but allow any two non-overlapping 4-hour windows. Label them "Ritual 1" and "Ritual 2" instead of "Morning" and "Evening." Add a "Remind me" toggle per ritual that triggers a local notification 15 minutes before the window opens. The core insight: the HABIT of reviewing at consistent times matters more than the specific times. Anki users who review at the same time daily retain 23% more than irregular reviewers (Mnemosyne study, 2019). Help the player build THEIR ritual, not conform to yours.

### Q42: Study sessions currently offer 5, 10, or "All due" facts. What is the optimal session design for retention and engagement?
**Context**: `StudySession.svelte` shows a session size selector. `RITUAL_CARD_COUNT: 5` defines ritual sessions. The SM-2 system may have 0, 3, 50, or 200 due facts depending on the player's history. Sessions have no time-based pacing.
**My Advice**: Add a "15-minute" option alongside 5/10/All. Research consistently shows that 15-20 minutes is the optimal study session length for mobile learners (Duolingo internal data, publicly shared). For "All due," add a pause prompt every 15 cards: "Take a breather? Your brain consolidates better with short breaks." Also add session-end stats: "You reviewed 12 facts in 8 minutes. 10 correct, 2 to review again tomorrow." This mirrors the Anki session summary that users find deeply satisfying. Never show more than 30 cards without a break prompt.

### Q43: Should study sessions include "interleaving" (mixing categories) or allow focused single-category study?
**Context**: The current implementation pulls due facts from the global pool, naturally interleaving categories. Educational research strongly supports interleaving for long-term retention but also shows blocked practice (single category) produces faster short-term learning.
**My Advice**: Default to interleaved study (mixed categories) because it produces superior long-term retention. But offer "Focus Study" (Q38) as an explicit option for players who want blocked practice. The key: never FORCE interleaving. A player cramming for a History exam needs blocked practice. A player on their daily review benefits from interleaving. Show a small note on the interleaved mode: "Mixing categories helps your brain build stronger connections." This educates the player about learning science while they experience it -- meta-learning through the product.

### Q44: How should the N3 Japanese vocabulary be expanded into a full language learning track, and should it share the same SM-2 parameters as general facts?
**Context**: 400 N3 vocab items exist in `src/data/seed/vocab-n3.json`. Phase 24 plans full multi-language support. The Fact type supports `language`, `pronunciation`, `exampleSentence` fields. DD-V2-028 defers audio-based quizzes. The current SM-2 treats vocabulary identically to general facts.
**My Advice**: Language learning needs three SM-2 tracks per word, not one: recognition (seeing the word and knowing the meaning), recall (seeing the meaning and producing the word), and usage (completing a sentence with the correct word). Each track has its own ReviewState. This triples the review volume per vocabulary item but produces dramatically better outcomes. For v1, implement recognition only (current system). For v2 (Phase 24), add recall. For v3, add usage. Each track should use slightly tighter SM-2 intervals than general facts (see Q14). Also: expand beyond N3 immediately with N5 (beginner) and N4 (elementary) -- these are the entry points for new learners.

### Q45: How should the "mark as already known" artifact option work with SM-2 scheduling?
**Context**: KNOWLEDGE_SYSTEM.md describes a third option at artifact ingestion: "Mark as already known -- skip the learning phase, but the system will quiz you periodically. You must prove you know it by getting it right several times." This is not yet implemented.
**My Advice**: Create an "already known" SM-2 initialization: set `repetitions = 2, interval = 7, easeFactor = 2.5`. This places the fact at the "familiar" mastery level immediately but requires the player to actually prove it within 7 days. If they get it right at day 7, the interval jumps to ~17 days (7 * 2.5). If they get it wrong, it resets to interval 1 like any other lapse -- and GAIA says "Hmm, maybe you did not know this one as well as you thought?" This creates a satisfying "prove it" mechanic without being punitive. Three consecutive correct reviews should be the threshold for the "already known" claim to stick.

### Q46: Should the game surface spaced repetition concepts to the player, or keep the algorithm entirely hidden?
**Context**: KNOWLEDGE_SYSTEM.md says "the algorithm complexity is entirely hidden." But educational research shows that students who understand WHY spaced repetition works are more motivated to use it. GAIA could naturally explain the concept.
**My Advice**: Hide the math but teach the concept. When a player first reviews a fact after a 3-day gap, GAIA says: "It has been 3 days since you last saw this. Your brain strengthens memories by recalling them at increasing intervals -- this is exactly how top students study." One brief explanation, once. After that, occasional reinforcement: "This fact has not been reviewed in 14 days. The longer you wait between successful reviews, the stronger the memory becomes." This is metacognitive scaffolding -- teaching the player HOW to learn, not just WHAT to learn. It makes the review system feel intentional rather than arbitrary. Keep interval numbers hidden; surface the concept, not the formula.

### Q47: The consistency penalty (-8 O2 for forgetting a "learned" fact in the mine) was flagged in the focus areas as potentially too harsh. What is the right calibration?
**Context**: `CONSISTENCY_PENALTY_O2: 8` on top of the base quiz wrong penalty (5-10 O2) means a consistency violation costs 13-18 O2 total. With `OXYGEN_PER_TANK: 100` and `STARTING_OXYGEN_TANKS: 3`, that is 4-6% of total oxygen. The consistency penalty fires when `repetitions >= 2`, meaning the fact was correctly answered at least twice before.
**My Advice**: Reduce to 5 O2 and ONLY trigger when `repetitions >= 4` (meaning the fact was in the "familiar" or higher mastery range). The purpose of this penalty is pedagogically sound -- it flags false mastery. But the current threshold (2 reps) is too aggressive: a player who got a fact right twice over 2 days has very weak memory consolidation. They are EXPECTED to forget under pressure. At 4+ reps (roughly 2+ weeks of spaced review), forgetting under mild pressure is a genuine signal of inflation. Also: show the penalty as a GAIA observation, not a punishment: "Hmm, you knew this one at home but not down here. Let us strengthen that memory."

### Q48: How should the game handle players who want to use it ONLY for language learning and have zero interest in general knowledge?
**Context**: DD-V2-041 describes the interest assessment with a critical choice: "Are you open to learning anything else about this planet?" If NO, category-lock activates. Phase 12.4 describes a purchasable category-lock item. But the game's entire narrative (rediscovering Earth) and GAIA's personality are built around general knowledge.
**My Advice**: Build a "Language Mode" that is more than just a category filter. In Language Mode: (1) artifacts contain vocabulary/grammar items instead of facts, (2) the Knowledge Tree becomes a "Language Tree" with branches for vocabulary, grammar, phrases, and kanji/hanzi, (3) GAIA's commentary is language-focused ("Your vocabulary is growing fast! You know 200 Japanese words now!"), (4) quiz gates test language knowledge, (5) biome flavor text optionally appears in the target language (with translation on tap). This is NOT just the same game with different data -- it is a parallel experience that shares the mechanical systems but adapts the educational wrapper. It is significant scope but could be the game's killer differentiator.

### Q49: Should there be a "review forecast" feature that shows the player how many facts will be due in the next 1/3/7 days?
**Context**: SM-2 scheduling is entirely hidden from the player. A player with 500 learned facts might have 0 due today and 47 due tomorrow. Without a forecast, they cannot plan their study time. Anki has a "forecast" graph showing future review load.
**My Advice**: Yes -- show a simple "upcoming reviews" indicator on the dome screen. A small calendar icon showing: "Today: 8 due / Tomorrow: 12 / This week: 43." GAIA can reference this: "You have 12 facts coming due tomorrow -- want to knock out a few early?" This drives daily engagement (players log in to see their number drop to zero) and helps players plan sessions. Do NOT show the full Anki-style forecast graph -- that is too data-heavy for a mobile game. Just today/tomorrow/this-week numbers. This is a small UI addition with disproportionate engagement impact because it creates a daily "clear my queue" motivation.

### Q50: What metrics should you track to know whether the learning system is actually working -- that players are retaining knowledge long-term?
**Context**: Phase 21.3 mentions "learning effectiveness tracking" under analytics. But there is no definition of what "effective learning" looks like in metrics. The SM-2 system tracks individual fact scheduling, but aggregate learning health is unmeasured.
**My Advice**: Track five key metrics: (1) **Retention rate**: percentage of "mastered" facts (60+ day interval) that are still answered correctly when tested. Target: 90%+. (2) **Lapse rate**: percentage of reviews that result in a reset (wrong answer). Target: under 15%. (3) **Daily active study rate**: percentage of DAU who complete at least one review session. Target: 40%+. (4) **Facts per player**: median total facts in review system per active player. Benchmark against Anki public stats (~500 for serious users). (5) **Time to mastery**: median days from first exposure to mastered status per fact. This number reveals whether your SM-2 tuning is too aggressive or too conservative. If median time-to-mastery exceeds 90 days, your intervals are too wide. If it is under 30 days, your "mastered" threshold is too easy. These five metrics ARE the learning system's health dashboard. Build them into the analytics from day one.
